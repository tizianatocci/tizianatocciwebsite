Tiziana Tocci

PhD Candidate at Institut Curie, Paris.

Young researcher enthusiastic about subjects at the interface between biology and informatics.
I am currently conducting my doctoral project at Institut Curie, under the supervision of Hervé Isambert and Pascal Hersen.
By using novel causal discovery algorithms developed in the Isambert laboratory, our aim is to unconver the causal-effect relationships that govern the complex biological systems under study.
Specifically, I analyze cell microscopy videos and single cell transcriptomics data in order to extract features/variables of interest and uncover the association and causality between these properties.
I am inclined to work in scientific international environments and to participate into the organization of scientific events (member of the Young Researchers' Seminars committee of my research unit).

My journey

I was born and raised in Rome.
Since middle school I have always enjoyed Maths and Sciences, therefore I continued my studies in a scientific high school.
It was soon clear to be that I wanted to study Engineering applied to healthcare and I obtained my Bachelor's degree and my Master's degree in Medical Engineering at Tor Vergata University of Rome.
During my Master's, I discovered my passion about programming applied to solve medical and biological problems.
I got familiar with Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Image Analysis while studying programming languages in university and with online courses.
I followed a Data Analyst Academy at Avanade and I worked a few months as a Data Analyst because of my interest towards data analysis in industry.
I decided to pursue a doctorate in medical/biological research because of three main reasons: I wanted to deepen my knowledge of data analysis applied to the medical field, I feel that research is a field where you never stop learning and I strongly attribuite a noble value to medical research.
Moreover, I decided to pursue my PhD abroad because I wanted to go beyond the borders of what was my world at the time.
I succedeed in becoming a PhD Candidate at Institut Curie, I am part of the EuReCa PhD Program (Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions).
In the future, I would like to work at the interface between research and industry in an international and stimulating environment.
